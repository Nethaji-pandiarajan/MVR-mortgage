Do I Qualify?

To qualify for a mortgage, lenders typically require that you have a debt-to-income ratio of “43/49.” This means that no more than 43% of your total monthly income (from all sources, before taxes) can go toward your new mortgage payment, and no more than 49.99% of your monthly income can go toward your total monthly debt (including your mortgage payment). VA and FHA loans allow for even higher debt ratios on a case by case basis.

What is a Pre-Approval Letter?

A preapproval letter is a document from a lender stating that the lender is willing to lend to you, up to a certain loan amount. While this doesn’t guarantee a loan offer, it lets the seller know that you will likely be able to get financing. Sellers frequently require a prequalification or preapproval letter before accepting your offer on a house, and including a solid pre-approval letter in your offer package helps your offer stand out from dozens of others!
